Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM)?

Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM) is a type of diabetes that occurs during pregnancy and affects how your cells use sugar (glucose).

How can I manage GDM?

Managing GDM typically involves lifestyle changes, monitoring blood sugar levels, and possibly insulin therapy as prescribed by your doctor.

What are the risks associated with GDM?

Risks include high blood pressure, having a larger baby, and an increased risk of type 2 diabetes later in life. Proper management can help mitigate these risks.

Can GDM be prevented?

While GDM cannot always be prevented, maintaining a healthy weight, eating a balanced diet, and staying active during pregnancy can reduce your risk.
