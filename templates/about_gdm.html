About Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM)

Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM) is a type of diabetes that can develop during pregnancy. It typically occurs around the 24th week of pregnancy and affects how your cells use sugar (glucose). GDM can increase the risk of high blood pressure and having a larger baby. It is essential to manage GDM to reduce the risk of complications for both the mother and the baby.

Management typically includes lifestyle changes, monitoring blood sugar levels, and, if necessary, insulin therapy. Proper management can help ensure a healthy pregnancy and reduce risks associated with GDM.
